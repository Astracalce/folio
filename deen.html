Getting Started with Arabic: A Roadmap !

A brother asked me:(itne saal Arabic language padhne k baad ap ka kya khayal hai How should we start studying the Arabic language, presumably from scratch?)

Even though I have written many times about it, I will mention some points regarding learning this beautiful language based on what I have learned from the books of scholars.First of all, a person must be able to read the Quran. He should learn the basics of tajweed and know how to recite the Quran.

Then he should study a basic textbook on Arabic with a teacher. The book should focus more on text and vocabulary than grammar. Like the famous book of V. Abdur Rahim (Madinah Books, Durusul Lugah Arabiyyah),During the study of this book, a student should not read any other book in Arabic at all! He should put all his effort into understanding the sentence structure, basic grammar, and vocabulary in this book.If you can't find any teachers, there are lectures available on YouTube. In English, Urdu, and Kashmiri on the same book. I have myself studied from the lectures of Asif Mehrali (Lqtoronto.com) (Lqtorontosupport on YouTube).

The author has himself written notes on this book (Key Facts) that are available with the book (Madinah Books for English-Speaking Students).After studying this book, one should revise it and then read the text as much as possible while keeping a dictionary handy.

Some dictionaries: 1) Hans Wehr, Arabic to English. 2) Al-Qamoos ul Waheed Arabic-Urdu. 3) AlMujam ul Waseet Arabic-Arabic

The most part during the beginning years of learning Arabic is to reflect on the Usloob/Style/ Structure of the Kalaam Sentence. Arabic needs to be learned, as was narrated by Arabs, not by Englishizing or Urduing it! There is no problem with relating some things to what you know from other languages in the beginning, but we must not forget that Arabic is different from other languages. It has its own style and way of using the words in the sentence. Vocabulary can be developed through text or book reading. While reading the book, one must use the dictionary so as to put the meanings in their proper context.

Some authors whose books are beneficial:
Abul Hasan Nadwi,
Ali Tantawi,
Mustafa Lutfi ManfalutÄ«,
Mustafa Amin,
Ali Jaarim,
Sulaiman Uyunialel.
